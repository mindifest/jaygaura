Making Tech Work Smarter for You

Sep 25, 2024

I’ve been a self-taught programmer for over 20 years, working on everything from solo gigs to large corporate projects. One thing that’s consistently stood out: things often could be done better—more efficiently, more cost-effectively. I’ve seen projects that could’ve saved hundreds of hours with just a bit more planning upfront.

Technology moves so fast that it’s hard to keep up with “what’s now possible.” New tools, methods, and systems constantly emerge, and it’s easy to miss out on advancements while keeping up with the day-to-day. This often leads to sticking with outdated processes that still “work well enough” but could be costing time and money.

That’s where I come in. I focus on automating processes—“making computers work hard for humans.” My goal is to help businesses unlock what’s possible today and avoid wasting resources on inefficient old approaches.

I love solving problems and finding elegant, efficient solutions. It’s frustrating to come into a project when it’s too late to fix things properly, which is why I’d rather be there from the start. Give me a challenge, and I’ll tackle it with creativity and experience, all while keeping communication clear so you’re confident about the project’s direction.

At the core of it all, I love what I do, and I’m always looking for ways to do it better.
